To Do

Amsterdam is a diverse and lively city with a wide array of things to do and see. From its picturesque canals and historic architecture to its vibrant neighborhoods, bustling markets, and vibrant nightlife, there is an abundance of experiences to be had. Visitors can enjoy leisurely bike rides along the scenic canals, explore the city's cultural heritage at world-class museums, wander through charming streets lined with shops and eateries, or take a boat tour to see the city from a different perspective. With its unique blend of old-world charm and modern vibrancy, Amsterdam offers a multitude of opportunities for travelers to immerse themselves in the city's rich culture and distinctive atmosphere.


Museums:

Visiting Amsterdam's museums is a must for art and history lovers, with world-class collections including works by Rembrandt, Van Gogh, and Vermeer. From the Rijksmuseum to the Anne Frank House, these museums offer a rich cultural experience with historic buildings and awe-inspiring artworks.


List of popular museums:

Rijksmuseum

This world-renowned museum is dedicated to art and history and houses an extensive collection of Dutch masterpieces, including works by Rembrandt, Vermeer, and Van Gogh.

Anne Frank House

This museum is located in the actual house where Anne Frank, a Jewish girl who wrote a diary during World War II, hid with her family from the Nazis. It provides a moving and educational experience about the Holocaust and Anne Frank's life.

Van Gogh Museum

As one of the most popular attractions in Amsterdam, this museum showcases the works of Vincent van Gogh, one of the most famous Dutch painters. The collection includes many of his iconic paintings, sketches, and letters.

Heineken Experience

Beer lovers will enjoy this interactive museum that offers an immersive experience into the history and brewing process of the famous Dutch beer, Heineken.

The Rembrandt House Museum

This museum is located in the former home of the renowned Dutch painter Rembrandt, and it provides a glimpse into his life and works. Visitors can see his studio, art collection, and personal belongings.

NEMO Science Museum

Coffeeshops:

Visiting coffeeshops in Amsterdam is a unique experience due to the city's relaxed laws on cannabis. Coffeeshops are establishments where customers can legally purchase and consume cannabis for recreational use. These cozy and laid-back venues offer a distinctive atmosphere, with a wide range of cannabis products available for purchase, including marijuana and hashish. Coffeeshops are popular among tourists and locals alike, providing a relaxed and social environment to enjoy cannabis in Amsterdam's liberal culture. It's important to note that while cannabis is tolerated in coffeeshops, it's still regulated, and visitors should follow local laws and guidelines when partaking.

Canal cruises

Canal cruises are a quintessential experience when visiting Amsterdam, known for its picturesque canals that wind through the city. A canal cruise offers a unique perspective to explore the city's historic architecture, charming neighborhoods, and iconic landmarks from the water. Visitors can choose from a variety of options, including classic canal cruises, evening cruises to admire the illuminated city at night, hop-on hop-off cruises for flexibility, small group cruises for a more intimate experience, or themed cruises for added fun. With informative commentary, cozy boats, and stunning views, canal cruises in Amsterdam are a must-do activity for tourists, providing a relaxing and enjoyable way to soak in the beauty and charm of the city's iconic canals.


List of popular Canal Cruises:

Amsterdam Canal Cruise

This classic canal cruise takes you on a scenic tour of Amsterdam's historic canals, passing by iconic landmarks such as the Anne Frank House, the Westerkerk, and the Skinny Bridge. You can choose from various types of boats, including traditional canal boats or modern glass-topped boats, and enjoy informative commentary about the city's history and architecture.

Evening Canal Cruise

For a romantic and atmospheric experience, an evening canal cruise is a must. As the city lights up at night, you can enjoy a leisurely cruise along the illuminated canals, admiring the beautifully lit bridges, historic buildings, and twinkling lights reflecting on the water.

Hop-On Hop-Off Canal Cruise

This flexible option allows you to hop on and off the canal cruise boats at different stops throughout the city, giving you the freedom to explore various attractions at your own pace. It's a convenient way to get around and see the sights, including museums, shopping areas, and other points of interest.

Small Group Canal Cruise

If you prefer a more intimate experience, you can opt for a small group canal cruise on a smaller boat. These tours usually have fewer passengers, providing a cozy and personal atmosphere, and often include extras like drinks, snacks, or even a private guide.

Pizza Cruise or Booze Cruise

For a fun and unique twist on the traditional canal cruise, you can choose a themed cruise, such as a pizza cruise or a booze cruise. These tours offer a fun and lively atmosphere, with the added bonus of enjoying delicious pizza or drinks while cruising along the canals.

The Red Light District

The Red Light District is a unique and infamous area in Amsterdam known for its distinctive atmosphere and history. Located in the city center, it is known for its red-lit windows where sex workers offer their services. The area also has a vibrant nightlife, with numerous bars, clubs, and restaurants. In addition to its reputation as an adult entertainment district, the Red Light District is also home to historical landmarks, including the Old Church and the Oude Kerk Museum, as well as interesting shops, cafes, and street art. Despite its controversial nature, the Red Light District remains a popular tourist attraction, offering a glimpse into Amsterdam's liberal culture and its approach to sex work. It's important to approach the area with an open mind and respect for the local laws and customs, as well as the privacy and safety of the sex workers who work there.
